Financial Awareness Basics

Why money habits formed today shape your entire life — and how small decisions matter more than you think.

Financial awareness means understanding how money works in everyday life — how it is earned, saved, spent, and grown. It is not about becoming wealthy; it is about making informed choices instead of impulsive ones.

Most families make money decisions based on habit, pressure, or incomplete information. This leads to avoidable stress. The good news? Learning even the basics can change a family's financial trajectory forever.

⚠️ Common money mistakes families make

  • ❌ Borrowing at high interest (30–40% per year on informal loans) for daily needs
  • ❌ Spending more than income each month with no budget plan
  • ❌ Falling for "get rich quick" schemes and investment frauds
  • ❌ Having zero emergency savings — one illness can cause a debt spiral
  • ❌ Never checking bank statements or understanding where money goes

Budgeting for Households

Plan where every rupee goes before the month begins.

What is a budget? A budget is simply a plan. Write down your family's monthly income, then list all expenses. Whatever is left is for savings and emergencies. A simple formula: Income − Fixed Expenses − Savings = Spending Money.

Real example: A family earning ₹25,000/month: ₹8,000 rent + ₹5,000 groceries + ₹2,000 school fees + ₹3,000 savings = ₹7,000 flexible spending.
✅ Do's
  • Write a budget every month
  • Track every expense
  • Review and adjust
❌ Don'ts
  • Spend before budgeting
  • Ignore small purchases
  • Skip savings "this month"
💡

Saving vs Spending

Understand needs and wants to make smarter choices every day.

Need vs Want: A need is something essential (food, medicine, rent). A want is something you desire but can live without (new shoes when old ones work, extra snacks). Confusing the two is how most households overspend.

The 50-30-20 Rule: 50% on needs, 30% on wants, 20% on savings. Even adjusting this slightly can transform your family's financial health.
✅ Do's
  • Save first, spend second
  • Wait 24 hrs before big purchases
  • Use a shopping list
❌ Don'ts
  • Buy on impulse
  • Use credit for daily needs
  • Compare with neighbours

Banking Basics

Accounts, ATMs, UPI, and how the modern banking system works.

Bank Account: A safe place to store money and earn small interest (2–4%). Always safer than keeping cash at home.
ATM: Withdraw cash anytime. Never share your PIN with anyone — not even bank staff.
UPI: Unified Payments Interface. Send/receive money instantly using just a phone number or UPI ID. Free and instant.
✅ Do's
  • Keep your PIN secret
  • Use official bank apps
  • Check statements monthly
❌ Don'ts
  • Share OTP with anyone
  • Use public WiFi for banking
  • Click unknown payment links
📋

Loans, EMIs & Interest

Understand what borrowing actually costs you in real money.

EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment): When you borrow money, you repay it in fixed monthly amounts including interest. A ₹1,00,000 loan at 12% annual interest for 2 years = ₹4,707/month. Total paid = ₹1,12,968 — you paid ₹12,968 as interest!

Rule of thumb: Your total EMIs should never exceed 40% of your monthly income.
✅ Do's
  • Compare interest rates
  • Read full loan agreement
  • Pay EMI on time always
❌ Don'ts
  • Borrow from moneylenders at 5%/month (60%/year!)
  • Take loans for wants
  • Miss EMI payments
🛡️

Insurance & Emergency Funds

Protect what you have built before life surprises you.

Emergency Fund: Save 3–6 months of your family's expenses in a separate savings account. This is your safety net so you do not need to borrow when an emergency strikes.

Health Insurance: A ₹5 lakh family health cover can cost as little as ₹8,000–₹15,000 per year. Without it, one hospital stay can destroy years of savings.
✅ Do's
  • Build ₹10,000 emergency fund first
  • Get PM Jan Arogya Yojana if eligible
  • Insure income earner's life
❌ Don'ts
  • Use emergency fund for non-emergencies
  • Buy insurance you don't understand
  • Skip health cover

Feature 5

Fraud & Scam Awareness

Learn to spot financial scams before they cost your family everything they have saved.

📞

Fake KYC / Bank Call Scam

Caller claims to be from your bank and asks for OTP or account details to "update KYC." Banks NEVER ask for OTP on phone. Hang up immediately.

📲

WhatsApp Investment Scam

"Guaranteed 50% returns in 30 days." No legitimate investment guarantees returns. These groups collect money and vanish. If it sounds too good, it is.

🎁

Lottery / Prize Scam

"You've won ₹5 lakhs! Pay ₹5,000 tax to claim your prize." You cannot win a prize you never entered. No legitimate lottery charges you to collect winnings.

🔗

Fake UPI / QR Code Scam

Scammers send QR codes saying "scan to receive money." Scanning a QR code SENDS money from your account — it never receives. Never scan unknown codes.

🏠

Chit Fund / Pyramid Scheme

Informal chit funds or "chain networks" that promise income by recruiting members. Early members profit; late joiners always lose. These are illegal.

🔍 Scam Check — Does this look suspicious?

Check all that apply to the offer you received. See if it could be a scam.

  • ✓
    They promised unusually high returns (more than 15% per year)
  • ✓
    They asked for an upfront payment to "unlock" your prize or loan
  • ✓
    They asked for your OTP, PIN, or password
  • ✓
    They said the offer expires very soon and you must decide NOW
  • ✓
    The contact came via WhatsApp, Telegram, or an unknown number
  • ✓
    You cannot find their official registered address or SEBI/RBI registration
Bonus Feature

Government Schemes for Families

Free and subsidised government programmes that most eligible families never claim. Check which ones apply to you.

Health

PM Jan Arogya Yojana (Ayushman Bharat)

Free health coverage up to ₹5 lakh per year for hospitalisation. Available to 50+ crore low-income Indians.

→ Check eligibility at pmjay.gov.in
Savings

Public Provident Fund (PPF)

15-year government savings scheme. 7.1% interest (tax-free). Start with as little as ₹500/year. Safe and sovereign.

→ Open at any post office or SBI
Insurance

PM Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana

Life insurance of ₹2 lakh for just ₹436/year (₹1.20/day). Renews every year. Any bank account holder aged 18–50 eligible.

→ Available at all major banks
Accident

PM Suraksha Bima Yojana

Accidental death or disability cover of ₹2 lakh for just ₹20/year. Renewable one-year personal accident cover.

→ Enrol via your bank account
Students

National Scholarship Portal

Central and state scholarships for students from Class 1 to PhD. Income-based eligibility. Apply once, receive directly in bank.

→ scholarships.gov.in
Banking

PM Jan Dhan Yojana

Zero-balance bank account with free RuPay debit card, ₹1 lakh accident insurance, and ₹10,000 overdraft facility.

→ Open at any public sector bank
